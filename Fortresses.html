Fortresses close the Danube

As the natural border between northern and southern Europe, the Danube was often the battlefield upon which many peoples such as the Celts, Romans, Byzantines, Turks, and Hungarians Serbs fought for supremacy. To strengthen their boundaries, were many fortresses were erected on the banks of the river. Some of these Fortresses still proudly recall the turbulent history of these areas.

Bač fortress

In the fertile plains of Vojvodina are the remains of the oldest Austrian fortress in Serbia. It is believed that she was built during the reign of King Charles Robert of Hungary. A tour of the Bač fortress, with its well-preserved Donjon (watchtower) and several auxiliary towers, evokes the atmosphere from the 14th century when the fortress walls were an important part of the The defense system of the Austrian Empire.

Petrovaradin Fortress

The fortress, built from 1692 to 1780, was owned by Austrian Military engineers on the system of French fortresses of the famous Master builder Sébastien Le Prêtre de Vauban designed. On a tour through the upper and lower town, past the arsenal and officer's pavilion, find out what life was like in this "city within the city". You can also discover the underground tunnel world of the fortress, in which a labyrinth of 16 km long corridors awaits you. In July every Petrovaradin Fortress becomes the capital of pleasure every year, as the famous Exit music festival takes place here.

Belgrade Fortress

Belgrade Fortress - Kalemegdan, one of the main attractions the Serbian capital, is a popular place for walking for Residents and visitors. The first fortress was built by the Celts. However, it owes its present appearance to the peoples who came after it. Between the 2nd and 18th centuries, Romans, Byzantines, Hungarians and Turks adapted the fortress to their own needs and left one behind Eclectic style structure.

Smederevo Fortress

Looking at the peaceful landscape of Smederevo, it is hard to believe that a few centuries ago two empires in one battle in this place met. Built in the 15th century, the fortress of Smederevo represents the last major construction of the Serbian military architecture. The fortress soon became the scene of many Serb-Turkish conflicts.

Ram Fortress

If you get anywhere near the village of Ram, you will be fascinated by the sight of the almost magical star-shaped fortress. The fortress built in the 15th century has witnessed many battles and numerous conquests before ultimately falling into medieval hands Serbian despot fell.

Golubac Fortress

On the right bank of the Danube, at the entrance to the Iron Gate Gorge (Đerdapska klisura), is another architectural gem. Golubac fortress (Golubačka tvrđava) was in the 14th century on an inaccessible Built on a cliff and was the scene of numerous conflicts between Serbs and Ottomans. The view of the Golubac Fortress from the lookout point is the same today fascinating as it was back then, when the medieval rulers met their next Plan strategic steps from a bird's eye view.
