Mountains

The highest mountain in the country officially managed in Serbia is the in Kosovo i Metohija is 2656 m high Đeravica. The highest point of the Central Serbia is the 2169 m high Midžor on the border with Bulgaria. The northern Serbian province of Vojvodina lies in the Pannonian Plain. Therefore the highest elevation of this part of the country is the 641 m high Gudurički vrh in the foothills of the Carpathians, near the border with Romania.

Rivers

The rivers of Serbia are among the strongest in the north of the country meandering mighty lowland rivers of the Danube lowlands, in the central, western and eastern area to the mountain rivers the Dinarides and Carpathians, and in Kosovo to the small lowland rivers. Only Sava, Tisa and Danube are navigable throughout, but also Route sections at the mouths of the Drina and the Great Morava as well as the numerous large reservoirs. The once widespread rafting on Drina and Morava has been around since 1960s set.

Fortresses

Today's Serbia is littered with the remains of dozens of fortresses that were built in strategically important places - on rivers, along the trade routes and at the borders of past empires.
